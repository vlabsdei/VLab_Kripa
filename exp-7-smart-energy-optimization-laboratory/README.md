### About the Experiment

The Smart Energy Optimization Laboratory is a virtual laboratory experiment developed under the SmartFactory 4.0 Virtual Lab project. This experiment introduces students to the principles of industrial energy management, sustainable manufacturing, and energy optimization techniques used in modern Industry 4.0 smart factories.

The experiment provides an interactive simulation where users can configure factory operating parameters such as machine load, operating hours, renewable energy contribution, battery storage, power factor, electricity tariff, and idle machines to analyze their impact on power consumption, energy efficiency, electricity cost, carbon emissions, and overall factory performance. Students gain practical knowledge of Industrial Energy Management Systems (IEMS), renewable energy integration, battery energy storage, and data-driven energy optimization strategies for sustainable manufacturing.
