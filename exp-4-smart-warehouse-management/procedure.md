# Procedure

## Step 1: Launch the Simulator

1. Open the Smart Warehouse Management Simulator.
2. The dashboard displays the warehouse digital twin, KPI cards, warehouse status panel, control panel, observation panel, and operation log.
3. Verify that the system initializes with default warehouse parameters.

---

## Step 2: Configure Warehouse Parameters

Adjust the following input parameters using the control panel:

### Storage Capacity

Represents the total warehouse storage space available.

### Incoming Orders

Represents the number of customer orders entering the warehouse.

### Robot Speed

Represents the operational speed of the Automated Guided Vehicle (AGV).

### Inventory Threshold

Represents the minimum inventory level required before replenishment is triggered.

---

## Step 3: Run the Warehouse Simulation

1. Click the **Run Simulation** button.
2. Observe the AGV robot movement inside the warehouse.
3. Monitor rack occupancy and inventory allocation.
4. Observe order movement from inbound dock to storage and dispatch zones.

---

## Step 4: Observe Warehouse KPIs

Analyze the following performance indicators generated by the simulator:

### Storage Utilization

Indicates how efficiently warehouse space is being used.

\[
Storage\ Utilization =
\frac{Used\ Storage}
{Total\ Storage}
\times 100
\]

### Order Processing Time

Indicates the time required to process incoming orders.

### Robot Utilization

Indicates the percentage of time the AGV is actively performing tasks.

### Warehouse Efficiency

Represents overall warehouse operational performance.

### Congestion Index

Represents the risk of warehouse congestion due to excessive storage or order load.

### Inventory Health

Indicates inventory stability and stock availability.

---

## Step 5: Analyze Warehouse Operations

Observe the digital twin visualization and identify:

- Rack occupancy levels
- Inventory distribution
- AGV movement efficiency
- Storage bottlenecks
- Dispatch performance
- Congestion conditions

---

## Step 6: Evaluate Inventory Threshold Conditions

Perform the following:

1. Increase incoming orders.
2. Reduce inventory threshold.
3. Run the simulation again.
4. Observe inventory alerts and warehouse behavior.

Analyze how inventory levels affect warehouse performance.

---

## Step 7: Evaluate AGV Performance

1. Increase robot speed.
2. Run the simulation.
3. Observe changes in:

   - Order processing time
   - Robot utilization
   - Warehouse efficiency

4. Compare results with lower robot speed settings.

---

## Step 8: Study Congestion Scenarios

Create congestion conditions by:

- Increasing incoming orders.
- Reducing storage capacity.
- Increasing warehouse occupancy.

Observe:

- Increased processing delays.
- Reduced warehouse efficiency.
- Congestion alerts.
- Inventory management challenges.

---

## Step 9: Review Observations and Recommendations

Analyze the automatically generated observations.

The simulator provides:

- Operational insights
- Congestion warnings
- Inventory recommendations
- Resource optimization suggestions
- Warehouse performance assessment
