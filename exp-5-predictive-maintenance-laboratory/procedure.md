# Procedure

1. Open the Predictive Maintenance Laboratory simulation.

2. Explore the **Machine Controls** to adjust the following machine parameters:
   - Motor Temperature (20 - 150 °C)
   - Vibration Frequency (0 - 100 Hz)
   - Operating Hours (0 - 10,000 hrs)
   - Lubrication Level (0 - 100 %)

3. Notice that as you adjust sliders, the health and risk calculations update in real-time. Click **Run Simulation** to animate the interactive SVG digital twin and start the live data logging.

4. Observe the **Machine Digital Twin**:
   - The Motor block's heat glow color based on temperature.
   - The rotating fan and impeller speeds based on vibration.
   - The fluid flow colors depending on lubrication levels.
   - The live sensor readouts and system pressure gauge.

5. Check the **Observations & Diagnostics** panel for:
   - Core System Health Gauge.
   - Condition Monitor Heatmap (Thermal, Vibration, Lubrication, Wear zones).
   - AI Maintenance Recommendations depending on the risk level.

6. Apply **Maintenance Actions** (Lubricate System or Full Overhaul) from the control panel and observe how the Maintenance Score and Health recover instantly.

7. Review the **KPI Trend Analysis** chart and **Operation Log** table as the simulation ticks. You can export the logs to CSV using the Export button.

8. Analyze how temperature, vibration, operating hours, and lubrication influence machine health across different configurations.

9. Complete the post-assessment after finishing the simulation.
