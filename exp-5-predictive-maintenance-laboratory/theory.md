# Theory

## 1. Introduction

Predictive Maintenance (PdM) is an Industry 4.0 maintenance strategy that predicts equipment failures before they occur using sensor data and machine condition monitoring. Unlike reactive maintenance, which repairs machines after failure, or preventive maintenance, which follows fixed schedules, predictive maintenance uses real-time data analytics to determine the actual health of industrial equipment.

Modern manufacturing industries install Industrial Internet of Things (IIoT) sensors on machines to continuously monitor operating conditions. These sensors collect information such as temperature, vibration, operating hours, lubrication status, pressure, and power consumption. The collected data is analyzed to estimate machine health and predict potential failures.

## 3. Industrial Sensors Used

### Temperature Sensor

Measures motor or bearing temperature.

High temperatures indicate:

- Bearing wear
- Motor overload
- Poor cooling
- Electrical faults

Typical Range:

20°C – 150°C

---

### Vibration Sensor

Measures vibration frequency generated by rotating machinery.

High vibration indicates:

- Shaft misalignment
- Bearing damage
- Loose components
- Rotor imbalance

Typical Range:

0 – 100 Hz

---

### Operating Hours

Represents total machine running time.

As operating hours increase:

- Component wear increases
- Efficiency decreases
- Failure probability increases

Typical Range:

0 – 10000 hours

---

### Lubrication Level

Lubrication reduces friction between moving parts.

Low lubrication causes:

- Heat generation
- Excessive wear
- Higher vibration
- Machine failure

Typical Range:

0 – 100%

---

## 4. Machine Health Monitoring

Machine Health is an overall indicator showing the operational condition of industrial equipment.

Health Levels:

- 90–100% : Excellent
- 70–89% : Good
- 50–69% : Warning
- Below 50% : Critical

---

## 5. Failure Probability

Failure probability estimates the likelihood of machine breakdown.

Higher values indicate increased maintenance urgency.

The simulation uses the following scientific formulas to calculate the machine state:

**Maintenance Score** = 5 + [ (Lubrication Level % / 100) × 10 ] - [ (Operating Hours / 10,000) × 10 ]
*(Score ranges from 1 to 15)*

**Raw Risk Index** = (Temperature + Vibration) / Maintenance Score

**Failure Risk (%)** = (Raw Risk Index / 2.5) 
*(Capped between 0% and 100%)*

**Machine Health (%)** = 100 - Failure Risk (%)

## 7. Scientific Concepts

### Temperature Effect

Increasing temperature accelerates equipment degradation.

### Vibration Effect

High vibration increases mechanical stress.

### Lubrication Effect

Proper lubrication reduces friction and wear.

### Operating Hours Effect

Long operating duration increases fatigue and decreases reliability.
