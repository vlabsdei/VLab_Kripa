# Conveyor Belt Automation Simulator

## Introduction

The Conveyor Belt Automation Simulator is a virtual laboratory experiment developed under the SmartFactory 4.0 Virtual Lab project. This experiment helps students understand the working principles of industrial conveyor systems used in modern manufacturing and automated production environments.

The experiment provides an interactive simulation where users can modify conveyor operating parameters and observe their impact on conveyor performance, efficiency, energy consumption, and motor behavior.

---

## Objective

The objective of this experiment is to study conveyor belt automation systems and analyze the effects of various operating parameters on industrial material handling processes.

---

## Learning Outcomes

After completing this experiment, students will be able to:

- Understand industrial conveyor belt systems.
- Learn automated material handling concepts.
- Analyze conveyor speed and transport time relationships.
- Study the effect of load weight on system performance.
- Understand industrial motor behavior.
- Evaluate conveyor efficiency and power consumption.
- Explore Industry 4.0 automation concepts.

## Experiment Parameters

### Input Parameters

| Parameter | Range |
|------------|---------|
| Belt Speed | 0.5 – 5 m/s |
| Load Weight | 1 – 50 kg |
| Motor RPM | 100 – 2000 RPM |
| Conveyor Length | 2 – 20 m |

### Output Parameters

- Transport Time
- Conveyor Efficiency
- Power Consumption
- Motor Temperature
- System Status

---

## Engineering Concepts Used

### Conveyor Velocity

v = rω

Where:

- v = Conveyor Velocity
- r = Pulley Radius
- ω = Angular Velocity

### Power Consumption

P = F × v

Where:

- P = Power
- F = Applied Force
- v = Velocity

### Transport Time

Transport Time = Conveyor Length / Belt Speed
