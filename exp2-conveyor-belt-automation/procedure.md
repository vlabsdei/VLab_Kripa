# Procedure

## Step 1: Launch the Simulation

Open the Conveyor Belt Automation Simulator in the browser.

---

## Step 2: Observe Default Parameters

Observe the default values of:

- Belt Speed
- Load Weight
- Motor RPM
- Conveyor Length

---

## Step 3: Set Conveyor Parameters

Adjust the conveyor parameters using the sliders provided in the simulation.

### Adjustable Inputs

1. Belt Speed (1 – 10 m/s)
2. Load Weight (5 – 50 kg)
3. Motor RPM (500 – 2000 RPM)
4. Conveyor Length (2 – 20 m)

---

## Step 4: Run the Simulation

Ensure the **Run Simulation** toggle is checked to start the live simulation. Use the **Record Data Point** button to log readings in the Lab Observation Log.

---

## Step 5: Observe Output Parameters

Observe the following live KPIs and predictive metrics:

- Throughput (units/hr)
- Conveyor Efficiency (%)
- Power Draw (kW)
- Motor Temperature (°C)
- Transport Time (seconds)
- Machine Health Score
- Predictive Maintenance (Thermal Risk, Overload Risk, Wear Index, MTBF)

---

## Step 6: Analyze the Effect of Belt Speed

Increase and decrease the belt speed while keeping other parameters constant.

Observe:

- Change in transport time
- Change in power consumption
- Change in efficiency

---

## Step 7: Analyze the Effect of Load Weight

Increase the load weight and observe:

- Motor temperature rise
- Conveyor efficiency variation
- Energy consumption changes

---

## Step 8: Analyze the Effect of Motor RPM

Modify the motor RPM and observe:

- Conveyor performance
- Stability of operation
- Energy requirements

---

## Step 9: Analyze the Effect of Conveyor Length

Change the conveyor length and observe:

- Product transportation time
- Conveyor workload
- System efficiency

---

## Step 10: Inject Faults

Use the **Fault Injection** controls to simulate industrial failures:
- **Trigger Motor Fault**: Observe the rapid increase in motor temperature, thermal risk, and drop in machine health.
- **Simulate Overload**: Observe the impact on power draw, efficiency, overload risk, and wear index.

---

## Step 11: Compare Different Conditions

Perform multiple simulations using different parameter combinations and compare the results.

Examples:

### Case 1: Normal Operation

- Speed = 2 m/s
- Load = 10 kg
- RPM = 800
- Length = 5 m

Expected Result:

- High efficiency and machine health
- Low temperature
- Stable operation and low risks

---

### Case 2: Heavy Load Condition

- Speed = 4 m/s
- Load = 45 kg
- RPM = 1800
- Length = 15 m

Expected Result:

- Higher power consumption
- Increased motor temperature
- Reduced efficiency

---

## Step 12: Record Observations

Record:

- Input Parameters
- Generated Outputs
- System Status (Running, Fault)
- Observed Trends in Waveform
- Predictive Maintenance metrics

---

## Result

The Conveyor Belt Automation Simulator demonstrates how conveyor speed, load weight, motor RPM, and conveyor length influence throughput, energy consumption, efficiency, and overall conveyor system health in industrial automation environments.
